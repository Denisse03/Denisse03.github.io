UX Design & Research

During my internship with Life-Health Science partner Riley Children Hospital I had the opportunity to research 10 healthcare apps across the world. I had to read various articles from the National Institutes of Health to find the information I needed. After reading over the articles I gathered the necessary information needed to then search for the healthcare apps to see if they were available to be downloaded and used by using the apple store, google play, and on internet search. The purpose for this research was to determine how many healthcare apps were still available to be used, so that it can help be examples of a blueprint for future healthcare apps.

Mobile Application Healthcare Research

During my Usability Research I created four tasks to be completed by ten different users to determine which application was more user friendly. I create two different types of script to be tailored for in person testing or virtual testing. These scripts help to stay on track without giving more or less information to the users. I also had users complete the SUS form to be later calculated using Excel. In my finding it was concluded that Adobe XD was more user-friendly than Figma.
